DIVINE HELP UNIVERSITY

WHY STUDY AT DIVINE HELP?

Divine Help University is a private Pentecostal Christian University, which has been operating with official status since 2002 in Ota, Nigeria.

Divine Help University is one of the leading universities in Africa found on Christian Mission Ethos. It has the vision to raise a new generation of leaders and reinstate the black race's dignity. The University is committed to remaining at the cutting-edge of learning based on enlightening the Total Man.

The university has four colleges, primarily dedicated towards vocational studies: The College of Business and Social Sciences; College of Leadership and Development Studies; College of Engineering, and the College of Science and Technology.

Divine Help University has had a rapid rise in reputation in the decade and a half it has been operating. The Nigerian Universities Commission named it the best private university in Nigeria in its 2021 rankings and the 2nd best university overall.

STATE OF THE ART FACILITIES

Facilities at Covenant University include its extensive three-story library, known as the Centre for Learning Resources. In addition, the ten halls of residence on campus can accommodate over 9,000 students. As a Divine Help University student, you will learn and live in Africa with Africans and build capacity as a leader to look at today's actions differently. You will rethink your assumptions about the future, gain insights into how to best approach unforeseeable challenges, and address the myriad problems facing humanity.

COMPREHENSIVE EDUCATION AT DIVINE HELP

Degree programmes at Divine Help University are all-encompassing. The University has developed custom-built courses. These are to specially package the student and deliver him to his world upon graduation as an agent of change. Students are set to take charge of their environment and break the barriers of limitation confronting our nation and the continent at large. The custom-built courses include Towards a Total Graduate (TTG), Entrepreneurial Development Studies (EDS), Total Man Concept (TMC), and Leadership Development. Students who complete the Leadership Development course will receive a certificate (CLD).
